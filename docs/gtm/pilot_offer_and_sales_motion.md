# Pilot Offer And Sales Motion

Date: 2026-07-06
Owner: Customer Discovery / GTM Lead
Status: started local/offline GTM packet

## ICP

Start with paid social agencies, creative ops teams, or media buyers managing 30 to 1,000 Meta creative variants per launch cycle.

Best-fit signals:

- launch work is currently spreadsheet plus Ads Manager busywork;
- approvals, naming, URLs, UTMs, placements, and source lineage cause rework;
- multiple roles touch one batch before launch;
- safe export or review acceleration has value even before direct publishing.

## First Offer

Sell the first slice of the SaaS promise as a workflow rehearsal, not production software access yet.

Offer name: Meta Creative Import Mapping Sprint.

Scope:

- 45-minute discovery call;
- synthetic demo using the local creative import workspace;
- operator walkthrough of one 100-row batch;
- campaign and ad set mapping review;
- explanation of the future zero-retention handoff into Meta creative folders or ad-account media assets;
- time-saved and trust-friction capture;
- written findings memo with pilot recommendation.

Out of scope:

- customer creative import unless G1 is approved;
- OAuth, tokens, app setup, sandbox calls, uploads, validate-only calls, live publishing, or spend changes;
- any claim that the product is already production SaaS.

## Demo Flow

1. Start from the agency's current launch workflow.
2. Show the synthetic v2 manifest and generated workspace.
3. Filter ready, needs-review, and blocked rows.
4. Open row detail and preview.
5. Assign a reviewer role and note.
6. Export local review state.
7. Show the non-executable platform preview and explain why every payload is blocked.
8. Ask whether safe export without direct Meta mutation is valuable.

## Discovery Questions

- How many Meta creatives do you launch per week?
- What breaks most often: naming, assets, approvals, URLs, UTMs, placement rules, identity mapping, or client review?
- Who owns final launch readiness?
- What would you trust this product to do first?
- What would still need to happen in Ads Manager?
- Would a safe preflight/export workflow be worth paying for before direct publishing?
- Who owns the budget for launch tooling?
- What result would justify a paid pilot?

## Pricing Hypotheses

Treat pricing as a hypothesis until buyer proof exists.

| Package | Scope | Hypothesis |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Discovery sprint | synthetic workflow rehearsal and findings memo | low-friction paid validation |
| Pilot setup | one redacted or approved workflow plus success criteria | only after G1 customer-data approval |
| Agency workspace | recurring workspace access and support | only after product, trust, and production gates |

## Pilot Acceptance Criteria

A paid pilot should not begin until:

- buyer and user are named;
- weekly creative volume is known;
- current process time baseline exists;
- success metric is written;
- data boundary is approved;
- support owner is assigned;
- kill/pivot trigger is agreed.

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
| --- | --- |
| "Can it publish directly?" | Not yet. The SaaS goal is import, map, QA, and eventually safer platform execution. The first safe offer proves the import and mapping workflow before direct publishing. |
| "Can we use real data?" | Only after G1 approval defines retention, redaction, storage, access, and deletion. |
| "Do you store all our creative files?" | The target architecture is no: originals should be handed off into the customer's Meta creative folders/media assets after approval, while Meta Importer keeps lineage metadata only. Real uploads still require G1-G4 approval proof. |
| "Why not just use Meta bulk import?" | The wedge is review, QA, mapping, approval, lineage, and safe handoff around bulk launch work. |
| "Is this production ready?" | No. The current product is an offline proof and rehearsal surface. |

## Evidence To Capture

- participant role;
- weekly creative volume;
- current tool stack;
- current prep time;
- top three pains;
- confusing demo moments;
- fields/previews needed before trust;
- buyer and budget source;
- willingness-to-pay range;
- safe-export value verdict;
- confidence score from 1 to 5.
